Helplines

There are several helplines available for those who may be at risk of suicide or experiencing emotional distress. These include:
- Aasra: 91-22-27546669
- Sneha India: 91-44-24640050
- Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-266-2345
- iCall: 022-25521111
- NIMHANS Helpline: 080-46110007

Counseling services

There are several counseling services available in India, both in-person and online. These include:
- National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences (NIMHANS): NIMHANS has a number of clinics and centers across India that provide counseling and treatment for mental health issues.
- YourDOST: YourDOST is an online counseling and emotional wellness platform that provides confidential counseling and support.
- iCALL: iCALL is a free telephone and email counseling service provided by the Tata Institute of Social Sciences. They also offer in-person counseling in Mumbai.

Mental health hospitals and clinics

There are several mental health hospitals and clinics across India that provide treatment and support for those with mental health issues. Some of the major ones include:
- National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences (NIMHANS)
- All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS)
- Christian Medical College, Vellore
- Jai Vakeel Foundation and Research Centre, Mumbai

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the current status of suicides in India?

    Suicide is a significant public health issue in India. According to the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB), there were 139,123 suicides reported in India in 2019, which translates to a suicide rate of 10.4 per 100,000 population. However, experts believe that the actual number of suicides in India may be much higher, as many cases go unreported or misreported.

  • What are the common risk factors for suicide in India?

    The risk factors for suicide in India are similar to those in other countries, and include mental illness, substance abuse, social isolation, financial difficulties, relationship problems, and access to means of suicide such as pesticides and firearms. There are also some unique risk factors in the Indian context, such as high levels of stress due to academic pressure, arranged marriages and dowry disputes, and the stigma attached to mental illness.

  • Which age group is most affected by suicide in India?

    Suicide affects people of all ages in India, but there is a higher incidence among younger age groups. According to the NCRB, the age group of 18-45 years accounted for 64.1% of all suicides in India in 2019. Within this age group, the highest number of suicides was reported among those aged 18-30 years.
